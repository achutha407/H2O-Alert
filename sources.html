Sources

Government Reports

Ministry of Jal Shakti – India’s central authority for water resource management, including groundwater and river conservation.

• NITI Aayog’s Composite Water Management Index (CWMI) – A detailed report analyzing state-wise water availability and usage trends.
• Central Ground Water Board (CGWB) – Provides extensive surveys on groundwater depletion and availability in India.

📝 Why it Matters?
These reports provide official and government-verified data, ensuring accuracy in tracking India's water crisis.

WaterAid India – Works on improving access to clean water and sanitation in rural India.

International Organizations

World Health Organization (WHO) – Studies on water sanitation, disease prevention, and access to clean drinking water.

• UN-Water – A global framework tracking water-related SDGs, policies, and international cooperation.
• NASA’s GRACE Satellites – Uses satellite data to monitor groundwater depletion and hydrological changes worldwide.
